# Muesli Landing Page Direction

## Working Thesis

Move Muesli away from the current stark "free speech" posture and toward a warm, Mac-native companion for private voice workflows.

The page should feel like:

- a cozy desk garden, not a manifesto
- local-first and trustworthy, without sounding defensive
- useful immediately, like Wispr Flow's dictation clarity
- story-driven and product-specific, like Granola's meeting-note narrative

## Visual Direction

Concept: **solarpunk speech workspace**.

The hero uses pixel-art language: sunrise, garden grid, a talking person, the actual Muesli-style floating capture icon, floating grains, and a note window that types pasted speech into a text field. The movement should be slow and tactile, with one clear product loop: speak, listen, paste.

Palette:

- warm cream, mint, and sky as the base
- tomato/apricot for friendly energy
- fern/leaf greens for privacy and local-first trust
- dark oat ink for contrast

## Messaging Direction

Current headline:

> Your speech should belong to you.

Supporting promise:

> Muesli is an open-source Mac app for dictation and meeting notes. Speak naturally, paste clean text, and keep transcripts close. Do not rent out speech to the cloud when it should be yours.

Core proof points:

- dictation anywhere on Mac
- automatic meeting capture without bots
- local-first/on-device posture
- open-source and inspectable
- prominent GitHub star count in the nav
- a plain comparison against cloud-hosted speech tools that upload first and make privacy a vendor promise

## Open Decisions

- New canonical domain: placeholder not set yet in the prototype.
- Whether the final deployment should stay static under `docs/` or move to a Vite build pipeline.
- Whether to commission/generated bitmap hero artwork after the visual language is approved, or keep the CSS pixel-art system for maintainability.
